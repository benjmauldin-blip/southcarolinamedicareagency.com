Frequently Asked Questions

What is final expense insurance and how does it work in South Carolina?

Final expense insurance is a small whole life insurance policy designed to cover end-of-life costs — typically $5,000 to $25,000 in coverage. It pays a tax-free death benefit to your beneficiary, who can use the funds for funeral expenses, medical bills, outstanding debts, or anything else. Unlike term life insurance, final expense policies do not expire as long as you pay the premium, and the benefit never decreases.

How much does a funeral cost in South Carolina?

The average funeral in South Carolina costs between $8,000 and $12,000, and prices have increased significantly in recent years. This includes the funeral home services, casket, burial plot, headstone, and associated fees. Without a final expense policy, these costs fall to family members — often unexpectedly and at the worst possible time.

Do I need a medical exam to get final expense insurance in South Carolina?

Most final expense insurance policies do not require a medical exam. Instead, you answer a few health questions and coverage is typically approved quickly — often the same day. Even applicants with serious health conditions like diabetes, COPD, or heart disease can often qualify for coverage. We work with multiple carriers to find coverage for clients with a wide range of health histories.

How much does final expense insurance cost in South Carolina?

Final expense insurance premiums in South Carolina depend on your age, gender, health, and the amount of coverage you choose. A healthy 65-year-old woman might pay $40–$70 per month for $10,000 in coverage. A 75-year-old man with some health issues might pay $80–$120 per month for the same amount. We compare rates from multiple carriers to find your most affordable option.

Is final expense insurance the same as burial insurance in South Carolina?

The terms are often used interchangeably. Final expense insurance, burial insurance, and funeral insurance all refer to small whole life policies designed to cover end-of-life costs. They work the same way — a fixed premium, a guaranteed death benefit, and no expiration date. The money can be used for any purpose, not just funeral expenses.
