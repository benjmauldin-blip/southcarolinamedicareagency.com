How Final Expense Coverage Works

A final expense policy is a small whole life insurance policy — typically between $5,000 and $25,000 in coverage. When you pass away, your named beneficiary receives the death benefit as a tax-free lump sum. They can use it for absolutely anything: funeral costs, burial, outstanding medical bills, credit cards, or any other expenses they choose.

Unlike pre-paid funeral plans, a final expense policy isn’t tied to any specific funeral home. Your family has complete flexibility.

Options for All Health Situations

Most healthy applicants qualify for level benefit coverage — full coverage from the first day with the lowest premiums. If you have significant health issues, you may still qualify for a graded benefit plan (partial coverage in the first 2 years, full coverage after) or a guaranteed issue plan (no health questions at all, though at a higher premium).
