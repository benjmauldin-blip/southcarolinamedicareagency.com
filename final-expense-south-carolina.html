What Is Final Expense Insurance?

Final expense insurance (sometimes called burial insurance or funeral insurance) is a type of whole life insurance policy with a smaller face value — typically $5,000 to $25,000 — specifically designed to cover end-of-life costs. It requires no lengthy medical exam, is easier to qualify for than traditional life insurance, and is built for individuals typically between 50 and 85.

The policy pays a tax-free, lump-sum benefit directly to your named beneficiary — usually a spouse, child, or other loved one. Because it's whole life insurance, your premium never goes up, your coverage never expires, and the policy builds a small cash value over time.

Final Expense Questions Answered

Simplified issue plans ask a few health questions (typically 3–10) but require no medical exam. If you answer those questions favorably, you're approved immediately with no additional underwriting. Guaranteed issue plans ask no health questions at all — anyone in the eligible age range is approved. Guaranteed issue plans typically have slightly higher premiums and a 2-year waiting period before the full death benefit pays out.
