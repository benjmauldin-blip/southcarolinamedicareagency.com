What Moving to SC Means for Your Medicare

The impact depends on what type of Medicare coverage you currently have.

✅ Original Medicare — No Change

Original Medicare (Parts A and B) works nationwide. Moving to SC doesn't change your Part A or Part B coverage. However, you may want to review your Part D drug plan and any Supplement plan.

⚠️ Medicare Advantage — May Not Work

Medicare Advantage plans are regional. If your current plan doesn't operate in South Carolina, you'll lose your coverage when you establish SC residency. You'll need a new plan immediately.

💊 Part D Drug Plan — Review Needed

Part D formularies and pharmacy networks vary by state and region. Your current plan may not have preferred pharmacies near your new SC address — a plan review is smart.

🛡️ Medicare Supplement — Usually Transferable

Most Medigap plans are portable nationwide. However, if you want to change your Supplement in SC, you may face medical underwriting — so think carefully before making changes.

Your Action Plan When Moving to SC

Follow these steps to make sure your Medicare is sorted before and after your move.

1️⃣

Check If Your MA Plan Covers SC

If you have Medicare Advantage, contact your plan immediately and ask if it has a service area in your new SC zip code. If not, you'll qualify for a Special Enrollment Period to switch plans when you move.

2️⃣

Update Your Address with Medicare

Notify Social Security and Medicare of your new SC address as soon as possible. Your address change triggers the Special Enrollment Period for MA and Part D if your current plans don't serve your new area.

3️⃣

Find SC Doctors Who Accept Medicare

We can help you identify providers in Lexington, Columbia, and the Midlands who accept your current Medicare coverage — and verify network status if you're selecting a new MA plan.

4️⃣

Review Your Drug Plan for SC Pharmacies

Part D preferred pharmacy networks vary. We run a drug plan comparison specific to your new SC zip code and medication list to make sure you're enrolled in the most cost-effective plan.
