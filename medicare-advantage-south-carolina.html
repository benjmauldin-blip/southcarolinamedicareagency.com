What Is Medicare Advantage?

Medicare Advantage Explained

Medicare Advantage (also called Medicare Part C) is an all-in-one alternative to Original Medicare. Offered by private insurance companies approved by Medicare, these plans bundle your Part A (hospital), Part B (medical), and usually Part D (drug coverage) into a single plan — often with extra benefits like dental, vision, hearing, and fitness memberships.

South Carolina has a wide selection of Medicare Advantage plans available in 2026. As independent agents, we compare every plan available at your address — from carriers like Humana, UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Wellcare, and others — to help you find the right fit.

Medicare Advantage vs. Medicare Supplement

One of the most common questions we hear is whether to choose Medicare Advantage or a Medicare Supplement (Medigap) plan. Here's a quick overview:

  • Medicare Advantage — Often lower premiums; includes drug coverage and extra benefits; uses network-based care (HMO or PPO); out-of-pocket costs vary by plan.
  • Medicare Supplement — Higher monthly premium; works with any Medicare-accepting doctor nationwide; covers most or all of Original Medicare's gaps; no network restrictions.

The right choice depends on your health, preferred doctors, and budget. We'll walk you through both options at no cost so you can make the best decision for your situation.
