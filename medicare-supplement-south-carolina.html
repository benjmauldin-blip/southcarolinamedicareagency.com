What Is Medicare Supplement?

How Medicare Supplement (Medigap) Works

Original Medicare covers about 80% of your approved medical costs — leaving you responsible for the remaining 20%, plus deductibles and other cost-sharing. A Medicare Supplement plan (also called Medigap) is designed to cover some or all of those gaps, giving you predictable, comprehensive coverage.

Unlike Medicare Advantage, Medicare Supplement works alongside Original Medicare — not instead of it. You keep Medicare as your primary insurance and use your Supplement plan to cover what Medicare doesn't pay. This means you can see any doctor or specialist in the country who accepts Medicare, with no network restrictions.

Medicare Supplement vs. Medicare Advantage

Both are solid options depending on your situation. Medicare Supplement gives you maximum freedom and predictable costs; Medicare Advantage often has lower premiums but uses networks and has variable cost-sharing. We explain both options clearly so you can choose with confidence — not confusion.
