What Is Medigap?

Medicare Supplement, Plain and Simple

Original Medicare pays 80% of most covered services. The other 20% is yours — and with no out-of-pocket maximum in Original Medicare, those costs can add up fast.

A Medicare Supplement (Medigap) plan is private insurance that picks up those costs. You keep your Original Medicare, and the Supplement plan pays the rest. You can see any doctor or hospital in the country that accepts Medicare — no referrals, no networks.

Because these plans are standardized by the federal government, a Plan G from one company covers exactly the same things as a Plan G from another. The only real difference is price — and we shop multiple carriers to find you the best rate.

  • See any Medicare-accepting doctor in the U.S.
  • No referrals needed to see specialists
  • Predictable monthly premiums and out-of-pocket costs
  • Coverage travels with you anywhere in the country
  • No network restrictions — ideal for frequent travelers
  • Combine with a separate Part D plan for drug coverage
Most Popular Plans

Plan G vs. Plan N — The Two Top Choices

🥇

Plan G — Most Comprehensive

Plan G covers nearly everything Medicare Supplement can cover. After you pay the annual Part B deductible ($240 in 2024), your Plan G pays 100% of Medicare-approved costs for the rest of the year.

  • Part A coinsurance & hospital costs
  • Part B coinsurance or copayments
  • Part A hospice care coinsurance
  • Skilled nursing facility coinsurance
  • Foreign travel emergency (up to plan limits)
🥈

Plan N — Budget-Friendly Option

Plan N offers strong coverage at a lower monthly premium than Plan G. The trade-off: small copays for some office and ER visits, and you pay the Part B excess charge if a doctor doesn't accept Medicare assignment.

  • Part A coinsurance & hospital costs
  • Part B coinsurance (with small copays)
  • Up to $20 copay for office visits
  • Up to $50 copay for ER visits (waived if admitted)
  • Foreign travel emergency coverage
