Common Questions

Medicare Questions, Answered

A Medicare insurance agent helps you compare and enroll in Medicare plans — including Medicare Advantage, Medicare Supplement (Medigap), and Part D drug plans. An independent agent like Mauldin Insurance Group works with multiple carriers and can compare all your options at no cost to you. Their fee is paid by the insurance company, not by you.
Medicare Advantage (Part C) replaces Original Medicare with a private plan that typically includes drug coverage and extra benefits like dental and vision, often with low or $0 premiums. Medicare Supplement (Medigap) works alongside Original Medicare to cover the 20% coinsurance and other out-of-pocket costs. The right choice depends on your health, budget, and doctors — and we help you figure that out for free.
You can enroll during your Initial Enrollment Period — a 7-month window centered on your 65th birthday. The Annual Enrollment Period (AEP) runs October 15 through December 7 each year. Special Enrollment Periods are available for qualifying life events such as moving to South Carolina or losing employer coverage. Call us before you decide.

Possibly — and it may be urgent. If you had a Medicare Advantage plan in your previous state, it likely has no in-network providers here. Moving triggers a Special Enrollment Period, but you typically only have 60 days from your move date to act. Contact us as soon as possible to avoid any gap in coverage.
Medicare Part D is prescription drug coverage. Most people on Original Medicare should enroll in a standalone Part D plan when they first become eligible to avoid a late enrollment penalty. If you choose a Medicare Advantage plan, drug coverage is usually included. We compare Part D formularies across all available carriers to match your specific medications.
Both Plan G and Plan N are popular Medigap plans that cover the 20% Medicare coinsurance. Plan G covers everything except the Part B deductible ($257 in 2026), making it the most comprehensive plan available to new Medicare enrollees. Plan N has a lower monthly premium but requires small copays at doctor visits (up to $20) and emergency room visits ($50 if not admitted). The better choice depends on how often you use medical care.
It depends on the plan type. Medicare Supplement (Medigap) plans allow you to see any doctor or hospital that accepts Medicare nationwide — no network restrictions. Medicare Advantage plans typically have provider networks, so your doctor must be in-network. We always verify your doctors are covered before recommending any plan.
Medicare Annual Enrollment Period (AEP) runs from October 15 through December 7 every year. During this time, you can switch from Original Medicare to Medicare Advantage (or back), change your Medicare Advantage plan, or join, drop, or switch a Part D drug plan. Changes take effect January 1. We recommend reviewing your plan every year — formularies, premiums, and networks change annually.
