Common Questions

Medicare Questions, Answered

Here are the questions we hear most often from South Carolina residents considering Medicare coverage.

What is a Medicare insurance agent and how can they help me?

A Medicare insurance agent helps you compare and enroll in Medicare plans — including Medicare Advantage, Medicare Supplement (Medigap), and Part D drug plans. An independent agent like Mauldin Insurance Group works with multiple carriers and can compare all your options at no cost to you. Their fee is paid by the insurance company, not by you.

What is the difference between Medicare Advantage and Medicare Supplement?

Medicare Advantage (Part C) replaces Original Medicare with a private plan that typically includes drug coverage and extra benefits like dental and vision, often with low or $0 premiums. Medicare Supplement (Medigap) works alongside Original Medicare to cover the 20% coinsurance and other out-of-pocket costs. Supplement plans give you the freedom to see any Medicare-accepting doctor nationwide but have higher monthly premiums. The right choice depends on your health, budget, and doctors — and we help you figure that out for free.

When can I enroll in Medicare in South Carolina?

You can enroll during your Initial Enrollment Period — a 7-month window centered on your 65th birthday. The Annual Enrollment Period (AEP) runs October 15 through December 7 each year. Special Enrollment Periods are available for qualifying life events such as moving to South Carolina or losing employer coverage. Missing these windows can lock you in for a year, so timing matters. Call us before you decide.

Does it cost anything to use a Medicare insurance agent?

No — working with an independent Medicare agent is completely free. Insurance carriers pay agents a commission, and your monthly premium is exactly the same whether you enroll through an agent or directly with the company. You get expert, personalized guidance at zero extra cost.

We currently represent 6 organizations offering 52 products in our service area, including Medicare Advantage plans, Medicare Supplement plans (Plan G and Plan N are most popular for new enrollees), and Part D prescription drug plans. I just moved to South Carolina. Do I need to change my Medicare plan?

Possibly — and it may be urgent. If you had a Medicare Advantage plan in your previous state, it likely has no in-network providers here. Moving triggers a Special Enrollment Period, but you typically only have 60 days from your move date to act. Contact us as soon as possible so we can help you find a local plan and avoid any gap in coverage.
